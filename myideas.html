Code Geass: Lords of the Rebellion




Story/Plot Details

For my first post, I've decided to create a campaign based off of the amazing anime series "Code Geass: Lelouch of the Rebellion". For those of you who haven't seen the anime yet, the story's set in an alternate version of modern day Japan. The world is much different from ours, consisting of mainly just 5-6 nations, the biggest of which is called "Britannia". The Britannian army has waged war on almost every country, and has taken control of most of the world. Their reign has spread like a cancer throughout the planet, and they've caused endless suffering both to their own subjects, as well as to the people who come from different nations. One such nation is Japan, which was conquered and populated by Britannia. The Japanese have been driven to ghettos and prisons, and are being treated as lesser human beings. Because of this, various Japanese people have come together to form a group of "terrorists" called The Black Knights, lead by a mysterious man named Zero. In this version of the story, Zero dies before the end of the war, sacrificing himself to save his sister, Nunnally, from the Britannian Emperor's right hand, Cornelia Vi Britannia. The Black Knights were able to establish a foothold on the freedom of Japan, but after Zero's death, they had to fight as hard as they could to keep that foothold in place, for Zero was not only their leader, but their greatest strategist. This is where the Player Characters come into the fray. The Black Knights have begun recruiting people into their ranks in order to increase the size of their army. The Player Characters are to enter the Black Knights' ranks, and begin climbing their way up the status ladder. As they do so, they must fight back Britannia, and finish what Zero started. They must free Japan and destroy Britannia.



System


Player Characters are split into two groups: Combatants and Strategists. Combatants are the ones at the front lines of the war, leading the army to victory via brute force and destruction. Because of this, their builds will usually focus on Strength or Dexterity, as those are the most useful stats for them to have. Strategists are the people pulling the strings, winning the war by creating great strategies for the Black Knights to employ, as well as cutting deals and making alliances with various other organizations and governments.


Pros and Cons for both Classes



Combatant class Pros:

  • Excel in Combat
  • Have bonuses in Charisma rolls when they must intimidate someone
  • Have large amounts of health

Combatant Class Cons:

  • Roll disadvantage during Strategy encounters
  • Have negative modifiers to charisma and other stats when not using intimidation
  • Have negative modifiers in intelligence

Strategist Class Pros:

  • Excel in strategy and diplomacy
  • Have advantage in rolls involving charisma, influence, or wisdom
  • Have high intelligence

Strategist Class Cons:

  • Roll disadvantage while in combat
  • Cannot perform acrobatic feats
  • Roll disadvantage when trying to use intimidation

Dice



Dice (Combat)

  • D20: Used for all stat checks. Also used for attacks involving Knightmare Frames
  • D12: Used for attacks Pure Strength.
  • D10: Used for a number of attacks involving strength-dex weapons. Also used to determine percentage chances for various rolls.
  • D8: Used for dex-strength weapons.
  • D6: Used for pure Dex weapons.
  • D4: Used for broken weapons or hand-to-hand combat

Dice (Strategy/Diplomacy)

  • D20: Used for stat checks and Knightmare Frame attacks
  • D12: Used for Heavy Unit Damage Output (Minigunners, Brutes, etc.)
  • D10: Used for Medium-Heavy Unit Damage Output (Snipers, Assault Units, etc.)
  • D8: Used for Light-Medium Unit Damage Output (Pistolmen, Knights, etc.)
  • D6: Used for Light Unit Damage Output (Swordsmen, Infiltrators, etc.)
  • D4: Used to determine wait time for supply drops
